# Aquapella
![[AquapellawithCamera.jpg|500]]
The Aquapella is a hand-held gestural instrument for exploring the unique relationship of liquid turbulence and musical expression. The device consists of eight conductive water-level sensors in a custom 3D printed container. As a musician moves the device, it generates a chaotic flow of water within the container and translates the motion into real-time midi signals for audio-visual interpretation. The Aquapella also features a static camera to augment visual performances through displaying the water's movement to the audience.

I made the Aquapella along with Professor [August Black](https://august.black/) at CU Boulder. We have submitted this paper and video featuring the Aquapella to the International Conference on New Interfaces for Musical Expression [(NIME)](https://nime.org/). While the paper and video are under review, both are required by NIME to be anonymous and thus do not currently feature our names.

## Mechanical Design
The Aquapella has gone through many iterations in its form. It started off as a clear plastic Tupperware container through many iterations has ended with a 3D printed mechanical container that is tailer made for its function.
